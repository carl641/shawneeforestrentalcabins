Why Shawnee National Forest?

Spanning over 280,000 acres across Southern Illinois, Shawnee National Forest is a hidden gem featuring dramatic rock formations, ancient forests, pristine lakes, and some of the most diverse ecosystems in the Midwest.

Garden of the Gods

One of the most photographed locations in Illinois, featuring stunning sandstone rock formations with breathtaking panoramic views of the forest.

~15 minutes from cabins

Rim Rock / Pounds Hollow

A beautiful recreational area with a scenic trail along dramatic cliff edges and a pristine swimming lake surrounded by forest.

~20 minutes from cabins

Giant City State Park

Named for the massive sandstone blocks resembling city streets, this park offers excellent hiking, climbing, and a renowned lodge restaurant.

~45 minutes from cabins

Shawnee Hills Wine Trail

A collection of award-winning wineries and vineyards nestled among the rolling hills of Southern Illinois, offering tastings and tours.

~25 minutes from cabins

Cave-in-Rock State Park

Explore the famous 55-foot-wide cave overlooking the Ohio River, once a hideout for river pirates. Beautiful views and picnic areas.

~40 minutes from cabins

Bell Smith Springs

A rugged natural area with canyons, natural bridges, and clear spring-fed streams perfect for swimming and exploring.

~30 minutes from cabins
